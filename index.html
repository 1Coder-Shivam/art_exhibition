Featured Artists

Raja Ravi Varma

Raja Ravi Varma (1848-1906) is widely regarded as one of India's most celebrated painters. He is known for his iconic and realistic portraits, as well as his depictions of mythological and historical scenes. Varma's artwork combined traditional Indian aesthetics with European academic realism, resulting in a unique style that captivated audiences. His paintings often portrayed Indian gods, goddesses, and mythological characters with a naturalistic approach, making them accessible to a broader audience.

Tyeb Mehta

Tyeb Mehta (1925-2009) was a renowned Indian modern artist known for his powerful and emotive works. Mehta is best recognized for his "Diagonal" series, which depicted fragmented human figures in intense and dynamic compositions. He explored themes of violence, suffering, and existentialism through his art, using bold lines, vibrant colors, and angular forms to convey a sense of tension and anguish. Mehta's work has had a significant impact on the modern Indian art scene and continues to be highly regarded internationally.

Amrita Sher-Gil

Amrita Sher-Gil (1913-1941) was an influential painter often referred to as India's Frida Kahlo. She is recognized for her innovative style and the exploration of her dual Indian-Hungarian heritage. Sher-Gil's paintings captured the essence of Indian rural life, showcasing the struggles and aspirations of the local people. Her works combined elements of European classical art with the vibrancy and warmth of Indian colors and motifs. Sher-Gil's art celebrated the beauty of India's diverse culture and brought attention to the social realities of the time.
